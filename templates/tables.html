Dataset Overview

ID Number: Not present in your data, but often included in similar datasets.

Diagnosis (Result): The target variable, where M = Malignant and B = Benign.

30 Features:

These are computed from the digitized image of the FNA and describe characteristics of the cell nuclei. The features are divided into three groups:

  • Mean: The average value of the feature.
  • Standard Error (SE): The standard error of the feature.
  • Worst: The worst (largest) value of the feature.
Feature Descriptions:
  • Radius: Mean distance from the center to points on the perimeter.
  • Texture: Standard deviation of gray-scale values.
  • Perimeter: Perimeter of the tumor.
  • Area: Area of the tumor.
  • Smoothness: Local variation in radius lengths.
  • Compactness: Perimeter² / area - 1.0.
  • Concavity: Severity of concave portions of the contour.
  • Concave Points: Number of concave portions of the contour.
  • Symmetry: Symmetry of the tumor.
  • Fractal Dimension: "Coastline approximation" - 1.
Each of these features is provided in three forms:
  • Mean: Average value across all cells.
  • Standard Error (SE): Variability of the feature.
  • Worst: Largest value observed.
